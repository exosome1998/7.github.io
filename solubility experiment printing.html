Variables

Independent: Water temperature

Dependent: Time to dissolve

Controlled: Mass of salt (10g), volume of water (200ml), stirring method, type of solute

Diagram

Draw labeled setup showing both beakers with thermometers, stirring rods, and stopwatch

Method

  1. Measure 200ml cold tap water (record temp) into Beaker A
  2. Boil water, cool to ~60°C, measure 200ml into Beaker B (record temp)
  3. Weigh exactly 10g table salt on weighing paper
  4. Add salt to Beaker A, start stopwatch, stir continuously at constant speed
  5. Stop timing when salt fully dissolved (clear solution), record time
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 with Beaker B (hot water)
  7. Repeat entire experiment 2 more times for reliability
