1. Distance and displacement are the same thing. (True or False?)
Answer: FALSE - Distance is total path length; displacement is straight-line distance from start to finish with direction.
2. A car travels 100m north, then 50m south. What is the displacement?
A) 150m
B) 50m north
C) 50m south
D) 100m
Answer: B - Displacement = 100m - 50m = 50m north (net position from start).
3. An object with constant velocity has zero acceleration. (True or False?)
Answer: TRUE - Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity. If velocity is constant, acceleration = 0 m/s¬≤.
4. According to Newton's First Law, an object at rest will:
A) Always start moving
B) Stay at rest unless acted upon by a force
C) Accelerate automatically
D) Move in circles
Answer: B - Newton's First Law (Inertia): Objects resist changes in motion unless a net force acts.
5. If you push a wall and it doesn't move, the wall pushes back on you. (True or False?)
Answer: TRUE - Newton's Third Law: For every action, there's an equal and opposite reaction.

‚ö° Speed
Speed = Distance √∑ Time

üéØ Velocity

Example Problem:
A runner completes a 400m circular track in 80 seconds.
Calculate: (a) Average speed (b) Average velocity
Solution:
(a) Average speed = distance √∑ time = 400m √∑ 80s = 5 m/s
(b) Average velocity = displacement √∑ time = 0m √∑ 80s = 0 m/s
(The runner returns to start, so displacement = 0)
Answer: Speed = 5 m/s, Velocity = 0 m/s

Acceleration

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity.
üìä Acceleration Formula
a = (v - u) / t
a = acceleration (m/s¬≤)
v = final velocity (m/s)
u = initial velocity (m/s)
t = time (s)
‚¨ÜÔ∏è
Positive Acceleration
Velocity increasing (speeding up)
Example: Car accelerating from rest
‚¨áÔ∏è
Negative Acceleration (Deceleration)
Velocity decreasing (slowing down)

Example Problem:
A car accelerates from rest to 25 m/s in 5 seconds. Calculate acceleration.
Given:
u = 0 m/s (starts from rest)
v = 25 m/s
t = 5 s
Formula: a = (v - u) / t
Calculation:
a = (25 - 0) / 5
a = 25 / 5
a = 5 m/s¬≤
Answer: Acceleration = 5 m/s¬≤

(b) Displacement:

(c) Average Speed:

(d) Average Velocity:
Velocity = displacement √∑ time = 60m √∑ 60s = 1 m/s east
Average Velocity = 1 m/s east

Newton's First Law of Motion

Law of Inertia: An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force.
üöó
Real-Life Example
When a car brakes suddenly, passengers lurch forward
Your body wants to keep moving at constant velocity
Seatbelts provide the force to stop you
üí° Inertia = resistance to change in motion | More mass = more inertia

Newton's Second Law of Motion

F = ma
The acceleration of an object depends on the net force acting on it and its mass.
üìê Formula Breakdown
F = m √ó a
F = Force (Newtons, N)
m = mass (kilograms, kg)
a = acceleration (m/s¬≤)
üí° More force = more acceleration | More mass = less acceleration

Newton's Third Law of Motion

Action-Reaction: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
üöÄ
Real-Life Examples

Swimming: Push water back ‚Üí Water pushes you forward

üí° Forces always come in pairs | Same size, opposite direction, different objects
