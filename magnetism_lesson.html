üß≤ What are Magnets?

Magnets are objects that can push or pull certain metals.

(They have a special invisible force around them)

Every Magnet Has Two Ends:

  • North Pole (N) - Usually red
  • South Pole (S) - Usually blue
  • Rule: Opposite poles attract, same poles repel

üëª Invisible Magnetic Fields

Magnetic Field is the invisible area around a magnet where its force works.

(Like an invisible bubble of pushing and pulling power)

How Do We See the Invisible?

  • Iron filings - Tiny metal pieces that line up
  • Compass - Needle points along the field
  • Draw field lines - Show the pattern on paper

üìè Rules for Drawing Field Lines

Rule 1: Direction

Lines always go FROM North TO South

Rule 2: Never Cross

Field lines never cross each other - they're like train tracks!

Rule 3: Closer = Stronger

Lines close together = strong field. Lines far apart = weak field

Rule 4: Curves

Lines curve smoothly - no sharp corners or straight angles

üí° Memory Trick

Think of field lines like water flowing from North to South - smooth and never crossing!

‚úèÔ∏è How to Draw Field Lines Step-by-Step

Step 1: Draw Your Magnet

Rectangle shape, label N (red) and S (blue) ends

Step 2: Start at North Pole

Put your pencil at the N end

Step 3: Draw Smooth Curves

Curve around to the S end - like drawing a smile!

Step 4: Add Arrow

Arrow points FROM N TO S

Step 5: Repeat

Draw more lines above and below - don't let them touch!

üåç Earth is a Giant Magnet!

Our planet Earth is like a huge bar magnet!

(That's why compasses work everywhere on Earth)

Earth's Magnetic Poles:

  • Magnetic North Pole - Where compass needles point
  • Magnetic South Pole - Opposite end of Earth's magnet
  • Surprise! Magnetic poles are NOT the same as geographic poles

üß≠ How Compasses Work

A compass is a tiny magnet that can spin freely.

(The needle is like a mini bar magnet)

Why It Always Points North:

  • Earth's magnetic field pushes and pulls the compass needle
  • The needle lines up with Earth's field lines
  • North end of needle points to Earth's Magnetic North
  • Works anywhere on Earth!
