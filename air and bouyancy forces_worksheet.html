Section 4: Short Answer Questions (12 points, 3 each)
Write your answers in complete sentences.
1. Balanced vs Unbalanced Forces: Explain the difference between balanced and unbalanced forces using an example.
Balanced forces are equal in magnitude but opposite in direction, resulting in no change in motion (e.g., a book sitting on a table). Unbalanced forces occur when one force is stronger than the opposing force, causing acceleration or change in motion (e.g., a ball being kicked).
2. Ship Design: Explain how the shape and design of ships allow them to float despite being made of dense materials like steel.
Ships float because they are hollow and filled mostly with air. The overall density of the ship (steel + air) is less than water. The large volume displaces a lot of water, creating a strong buoyant force that equals the ship's weight.
3. Wing Design: Describe how the shape of airplane wings creates lift and keeps planes in the air.
Airplane wings have a curved shape (airfoil) that causes air to move faster over the top surface than the bottom. This creates lower pressure above the wing and higher pressure below, resulting in an upward lift force that supports the aircraft's weight.
4. Hot Air Balloons: Explain how hot air balloons work and why they rise when the air inside is heated.
Hot air balloons work because heating air makes it expand and become less dense than the surrounding cool air. The cooler, denser air outside creates a buoyant force that pushes up on the balloon. When this upward force exceeds the balloon's weight, it rises.
Section 5: Problem Solving (8 points)
Read the scenario and answer the questions.
Scenario: A submarine needs to dive underwater, stay at a constant depth, and then surface.
a) What must happen to the forces acting on the submarine for it to dive? (2 points)
The submarine's weight must become greater than the buoyant force. This creates unbalanced forces that cause the submarine to sink downward.
b) How does a submarine control its buoyancy to dive or surface? (3 points)
Submarines control buoyancy using ballast tanks. To dive, they pump water into the tanks, making the submarine heavier. To surface, they pump air into the tanks, forcing water out and making the submarine lighter.
c) What forces are acting on the submarine when it travels at constant depth? (3 points)
When traveling at constant depth, the submarine has balanced forces: weight (downward) equals buoyant force (upward). The submarine also has thrust (forward) balanced by drag (backward) for constant speed.
