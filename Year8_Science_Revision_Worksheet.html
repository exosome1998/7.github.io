PHYSICAL WORLD - Energy
1. Which form of energy does a moving car possess?
A) Potential B) Kinetic C) Chemical D) Thermal
B) Kinetic energy
2. A book on a shelf 2m high has 20J of gravitational potential energy. What happens to this energy when it falls?
It converts to kinetic energy (then sound and thermal energy when it hits the ground)
3. State the Law of Conservation of Energy.
Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another
4. Identify the energy transformation in a light bulb:
Electrical energy → Light energy + Heat energy
5. Draw the circuit diagram symbol for: (a) battery (b) resistor (c) switch
(a) Two parallel lines (one longer) (b) Rectangle (c) Break in line with diagonal connector
6. A device uses 1200J of energy and produces 900J of useful energy. Calculate its efficiency.
Efficiency = (900/1200) × 100 = 75%
7. Name THREE ways energy is transferred.
Conduction, convection, radiation (or mechanically, electrically, by heating)
BIOLOGICAL WORLD - Body Systems
8. Where does chemical digestion of carbohydrates BEGIN?
A) Stomach B) Mouth C) Small intestine D) Oesophagus
B) Mouth (enzyme amylase in saliva)
9. What is the function of villi in the small intestine?
Increase surface area for absorption of nutrients into bloodstream
10. Name the enzyme that breaks down fats.
Lipase
11. Describe the pathway of blood through the heart (4 chambers).
Right atrium → Right ventricle → Lungs → Left atrium → Left ventricle → Body
12. What is the function of red blood cells?
Transport oxygen (using haemoglobin) from lungs to body cells
13. Explain gas exchange in alveoli.
Oxygen diffuses from alveoli into blood; CO₂ diffuses from blood into alveoli
14. What is the main function of the kidneys?
Filter blood to remove waste (urea) and excess water/salts; produce urine
15. Name THREE components of the musculoskeletal system.
Bones, muscles, tendons (also: ligaments, cartilage, joints)
16. Muscles work in pairs. What are these pairs called?
A) Complementary B) Antagonistic C) Parallel D) Reciprocal
B) Antagonistic pairs
CHEMICAL WORLD - Matter & Atoms
17. Name the THREE states of matter.
Solid, Liquid, Gas
18. What is the process called when a liquid changes to a gas?
A) Melting B) Freezing C) Evaporation D) Condensation
C) Evaporation (or vaporisation/boiling)
19. Give TWO examples of physical changes.
Melting ice, dissolving sugar, cutting paper, breaking glass (any two)
20. Give TWO examples of chemical changes.
Burning wood, rusting iron, cooking egg, digesting food (any two)
21. What is the difference between an element and a compound?
Element: one type of atom only. Compound: two or more elements chemically bonded
22. Classify: H₂O, Fe, NaCl, O₂ as element or compound.
H₂O = compound, Fe = element, NaCl = compound, O₂ = element
23. What is a mixture? Give an example.
Two or more substances mixed but NOT chemically bonded. E.g. air, seawater, salad
24. State THREE properties of metals.
Conduct electricity, conduct heat, shiny/lustrous, malleable, ductile (any three)
25. What particles make up an atom?
Protons (positive), Neutrons (neutral), Electrons (negative)
EARTH & SPACE
26. List the layers of Earth from outside to centre.
Crust → Mantle → Outer core → Inner core
27. Which layer is liquid?
A) Crust B) Mantle C) Outer core D) Inner core
C) Outer core (liquid iron/nickel)
28. Define weathering.
The breaking down of rocks into smaller pieces by physical, chemical or biological processes
29. What is the difference between weathering and erosion?
Weathering = breaking down rocks in place. Erosion = transport of weathered material by wind/water/ice
30. Name the THREE types of rocks.
Igneous, Sedimentary, Metamorphic
31. How are igneous rocks formed?
Cooling and solidification of magma (underground) or lava (above ground)
32. Describe how sedimentary rocks form.
Sediments deposited in layers, compacted and cemented together over time
33. What conditions create metamorphic rocks?
High heat and/or pressure (without melting) transform existing rocks
34. Give an example of each rock type.
Igneous: granite/basalt; Sedimentary: sandstone/limestone; Metamorphic: marble/slate
WORKING SCIENTIFICALLY
35. What are the THREE variables in a scientific experiment?
Independent (changed), Dependent (measured), Controlled (kept constant)
36. A student investigates how temperature affects plant growth. Identify: IV, DV.
IV = Temperature; DV = Plant growth (height/mass)
37. What should always go on the x-axis of a graph?
A) Dependent variable B) Independent variable C) Controlled variable D) Any variable
B) Independent variable
38. When drawing a line graph, should you join the dots or draw a line of best fit?
Draw a line (or curve) of best fit through the data points
39. Data table: Time(s): 0, 10, 20, 30 | Temp(°C): 20, 30, 40, 50. What is the rate of temperature increase per second?
1°C per second (30°C increase ÷ 30s = 1°C/s)
40. Why do we repeat experiments?
To improve reliability/accuracy and identify anomalies (outliers)
41. What is a hypothesis?
A testable prediction about the relationship between variables
APPLICATION QUESTIONS
42. A solar panel converts 180J of light energy into 45J of electrical energy. Calculate efficiency and explain where the "lost" energy goes.
Efficiency = (45/180) × 100 = 25%. Lost energy becomes heat (thermal energy)
43. Why can't humans digest cellulose but can digest starch?
Humans lack the enzyme (cellulase) to break down cellulose bonds; we have amylase for starch
44. Explain why breathing rate increases during exercise.
Muscles need more oxygen for cellular respiration to produce energy; more CO₂ needs removal
45. A river carries sediment to the ocean. Link this to weathering, erosion, and rock formation.
Weathering breaks rocks → Erosion transports sediment via river → Deposition → Compaction/cementation forms sedimentary rock
46. Is rusting a physical or chemical change? Justify your answer.
Chemical change - new substance (iron oxide) formed; irreversible; requires oxygen
47. Draw a series circuit with 1 battery, 2 bulbs, and 1 switch.
[Simple diagram: battery → switch → bulb → bulb → back to battery in one loop]
48. Water is boiled then cooled back to ice. What state changes occurred? Are these physical or chemical?
Liquid→gas (evaporation), gas→liquid (condensation), liquid→solid (freezing). All physical changes
49. Explain the relationship between the circulatory and respiratory systems.
Respiratory system exchanges O₂/CO₂ in lungs; circulatory system transports these gases via blood to/from body cells
50. An object has 500J of GPE at the top of a ramp. At the bottom it has 400J of KE. Where did 100J go? Does this violate the Law of Conservation of Energy?
100J converted to heat/sound (friction). No violation - energy transformed, not lost; total = 500J
