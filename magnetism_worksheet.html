1. What are the two ends of a magnet called?
a) Top and bottom
b) North and South poles
c) Positive and negative

Answer:
b) North and South poles
2. What happens when two north poles are brought together?
a) They attract each other
b) They repel each other
c) Nothing happens
d) They stick together
Answer:
b) They repel each other
3. Magnetic field lines always point from:
a) South to North pole
b) North to South pole
c) Left to right
d) Any direction
Answer:
b) North to South pole
4. What does a compass needle point towards?
a) The sun
b) The nearest magnet
c) Earth's magnetic north pole
d) The moon
Answer:
c) Earth's magnetic north pole
5. Where is the magnetic field strongest around a bar magnet?
a) In the middle
b) At the poles
c) Far away from it
d) It's the same everywhere
Answer:
b) At the poles
6. Magnetic field lines can:
a) Cross each other
b) Never cross each other
c) Only cross at right angles
d) Cross only at the poles
Answer:
b) Never cross each other

Section 3: Short-Answer Questions (6 points, 3 each)
Write answers on the lines provided.
1. Magnetic Field Rules: List three important rules for drawing magnetic field lines.
1. Lines go from North to South pole
2. Lines never cross each other
3. Lines closer together show stronger magnetic field
2. Compass Explanation: Explain why a compass needle always points north.
A compass needle is a small magnet that aligns with Earth's magnetic field. The needle points towards Earth's magnetic north pole because Earth acts like a giant magnet.
