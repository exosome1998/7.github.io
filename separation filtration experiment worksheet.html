Method

1. Add 1 spatula sand + 1 spatula salt to 250mL beaker
2. Add 50mL water to dissolve salt
3. Stir mixture with glass rod for 5 minutes until salt dissolves
4. Fold filter paper: fold in half, fold again, open to cone shape (3 layers one side, 1 other)
5. Set up filtration: secure funnel in ring clamp, place filter paper in funnel, position conical flask below
6. Pour mixture slowly along glass rod into filter. Sand (residue) stays in filter, salt water (filtrate) passes through
7. Set up evaporation: place evaporation basin on tripod with gauze mat, position Bunsen burner below
8. Pour filtrate into evaporation basin. Heat gently until ⅔ water evaporates. STOP BEFORE DRY
9. Turn off burner. Let basin cool on bench. Salt crystals form as remaining water evaporates (24-48 hours)
⚠️ SAFETY: Wear goggles at all times. Stand while heating. Never touch hot equipment. Keep distance from flame.

Key Scientific Terms

Mixture: Substances combined but not chemically bonded

Filtration: Separating insoluble solids from liquids

Residue: Solid left in filter (sand)

Filtrate: Liquid passing through filter (salt water)

Evaporation: Liquid to gas, leaving solids behind

Soluble: Dissolves in solvent (salt in water)

Insoluble: Does not dissolve (sand in water)

Solution: Substance dissolved in another

Crystallization: Solid crystals forming from solution
