Subtopic 0: Scalar and Vector Quantities

Start with basic concepts: scalars vs. vectors, including distance/displacement and speed/velocity.

Step 1: Scalars and Vectors

Scalar: Quantity with magnitude only (e.g., distance, speed, mass).

Vector: Quantity with magnitude and direction (e.g., displacement, velocity, force).

Click for breakdown:

  • Distance: Total path length (scalar).
  • Displacement: Shortest path from start to end with direction (vector).
  • Speed: Distance per time (scalar).
  • Velocity: Displacement per time (vector).

Subtopic 1: Acceleration Calculations

Building on velocity, calculate acceleration as change in velocity over time.

Step 1: Understanding Acceleration

Acceleration: Change in velocity over time. Vector; positive/negative based on direction.

a = (v - u) / t

Click for breakdown:

  • a = acceleration (m/s¬≤), v = final velocity, u = initial velocity, t = time (s).
  • For constant speed but direction change, a ‚â† 0 (e.g., circular motion).

Step 1: Newton's Laws Overview

First Law (Inertia): Objects stay at rest or in motion unless acted on by a force.

Second Law: F = m a (force = mass √ó acceleration).

Third Law: Every action has an equal/opposite reaction.

Subtopic 3: Friction and Balanced Forces

Explore how opposing forces like friction balance or unbalance motion.

Step 1: Friction and Balance

Balanced Forces: Net force = 0, no acceleration (e.g., constant speed).

Friction: Opposing force between surfaces; static (prevents start), kinetic (during motion).
