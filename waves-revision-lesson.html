C) Stays the same
D) Doubles
Answer: B - Wavelength decreases because v = fλ (if v is constant and f increases, λ must decrease).
3. Sound travels faster in water than in air. (True or False?)

What is a Wave?

A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another without transferring matter.
🔑 Key Point
Energy travels through the wave, but particles only vibrate in place
Like a stadium wave - people go up and down, but the wave moves around

Wave Properties

📏
Wavelength (λ)
Distance between two consecutive crests (or troughs)
Measured in metres (m)
Symbol: λ (lambda)
⚡
Frequency (f)
Number of waves passing a point per second
Measured in Hertz (Hz) or waves per second
Higher frequency = more waves per second
📊
Amplitude (A)
Maximum displacement from rest position
Related to energy: larger amplitude = more energy
Loud sounds have large amplitude; quiet sounds have small amplitude
🚀
Wave Speed (v)
How fast the wave travels through the medium
Measured in metres per second (m/s)
Depends on the medium the wave travels through

The Wave Equation

The relationship between wave speed, frequency, and wavelength:
📐 Wave Equation
v = f × λ
v = wave speed (m/s)
f = frequency (Hz)
λ = wavelength (m)
💡 If speed is constant: increasing frequency → decreasing wavelength

Wave Equation: Practice Problems

Problem 1: Find Wave Speed
A wave has frequency 50 Hz and wavelength 2 m. Calculate the wave speed.
Given:
f = 50 Hz
λ = 2 m
Formula: v = f × λ
Calculation:
v = 50 Hz × 2 m
v = 100 m/s
Answer: Wave speed = 100 m/s
Problem 2: Find Frequency
Sound waves travel at 340 m/s with wavelength 0.5 m. Calculate frequency.
Given:
v = 340 m/s
λ = 0.5 m
Rearrange: f = v ÷ λ
Calculation:
f = 340 m/s ÷ 0.5 m
f = 680 Hz
Answer: Frequency = 680 Hz

Wave Equation: More Practice

Problem 3: Find Wavelength
Radio waves travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s with frequency 100 MHz (100 × 10⁶ Hz). Find wavelength.
Given:
v = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
f = 100 × 10⁶ Hz = 1 × 10⁸ Hz
Rearrange: λ = v ÷ f
Calculation:
λ = (3 × 10⁸) ÷ (1 × 10⁸)
λ = 3 m
Answer: Wavelength = 3 m
Problem 4: Inverse Relationship
A microwave has wavelength 0.12 m. If frequency doubles (speed constant), find new wavelength.
Key concept: v = f × λ
If v is constant and f doubles, then λ must halve!
Calculation:
New λ = 0.12 m ÷ 2
New λ = 0.06 m
Answer: New wavelength = 0.06 m (6 cm)

Types of Waves

🔊 Mechanical Waves
Require a medium to travel (solid, liquid, or gas)
Cannot travel through a vacuum
Examples: sound, water waves, seismic waves
💡 Electromagnetic Waves
Do NOT require a medium
Can travel through a vacuum (space)
All travel at the speed of light: 3 × 10⁸ m/s
Examples: light, radio waves, X-rays, microwaves

The Electromagnetic Spectrum

All electromagnetic waves travel at the same speed (3 × 10⁸ m/s) but have different frequencies and wavelengths.
💡 Remember: Radio waves have longest wavelength; Gamma rays have shortest wavelength

Electromagnetic Spectrum: Uses

📻
Radio Waves
Broadcasting: TV, radio, mobile phones
Longest wavelength, lowest frequency
Low energy, safe for communication
📡
Microwaves
Communication: satellites, WiFi, mobile networks
Cooking: microwave ovens heat water molecules
Wavelength: 1 mm to 1 m
🌡️
Infrared
Thermal imaging, night vision cameras
Remote controls, heating
All warm objects emit infrared radiation
🌈
Visible Light
The only part humans can see!
Range: Red (long λ) to Violet (short λ)
Photography, vision, photosynthesis

More Electromagnetic Uses

☀️
Ultraviolet (UV)
Sterilization: kills bacteria
Sun tanning, vitamin D production
⚠️ Danger: Can cause skin cancer and eye damage
🏥
X-rays
Medical imaging: see bones, detect fractures
Airport security scanners
⚠️ High energy: can damage cells, use sparingly
☢️
Gamma Rays
Cancer treatment: radiotherapy
Sterilizing medical equipment
⚠️ Most dangerous: highest energy, most penetrating
💡 Higher frequency = higher energy = more dangerous to living tissue

How Particle Arrangement Affects Wave Speed

The speed of mechanical waves depends on how particles are arranged in the medium.
🔑 Key Principle
Closer particles = faster energy transfer
Vibrations pass more quickly between tightly-packed particles
Sound speed: Solids > Liquids > Gases

Sound Speed in Different Media

🔊 Sound Speed Comparison
Air (gas): ~340 m/s
Water (liquid): ~1,500 m/s (4× faster!)
Steel (solid): ~6,000 m/s (17× faster!)
Why does sound travel faster in water?

Water particles are closer together than air particles, so vibrations transfer more efficiently from particle to particle.
💡 Dolphins and whales use sound underwater because it travels far and fast!

Energy Transfer in Waves

Waves transfer energy from one place to another without transferring matter.
🔊
Sound Energy
Vibrations transfer through air particles
Your ear detects these vibrations as sound
Example: Speaker → Air → Eardrum
☀️
Radiant Energy (Light)
Electromagnetic waves carry energy
Can travel through vacuum (no medium needed)
Example: Sun → Space → Earth (warmth and light)
🌊
Water Waves
Energy moves across water surface
Water particles move in circles, but don't travel forward
Example: Earthquake → Ocean wave → Shore
💡 Higher amplitude = more energy transferred

Quick Quiz 🎯

Q1: What is the formula linking wave speed, frequency, and wavelength?
Answer: v = f × λ (speed = frequency × wavelength)
Q2: A wave has frequency 200 Hz and wavelength 1.5 m. Calculate wave speed.
Answer: v = 200 × 1.5 = 300 m/s
Q3: Why does sound travel faster in water than in air?
Answer: Water particles are closer together, allowing vibrations to transfer more efficiently.
Q4: Which part of the EM spectrum has the highest frequency?
Answer: Gamma rays have the highest frequency (and shortest wavelength).
Q5: What type of wave is used for mobile phone communication?
Answer: Microwaves (and radio waves) are used for wireless communication.

Summary

Key Concepts 📚

Wavelength (λ): Distance between crests/troughs
Frequency (f): Number of waves per second (Hz)
Wave Equation: v = f × λ
Inverse relationship: ↑ frequency = ↓ wavelength (constant speed)
EM Spectrum: Radio → Microwave → IR → Visible → UV → X-ray → Gamma
Sound speed: Solids > Liquids > Gases (closer particles = faster)
Energy transfer: Waves carry energy without transferring matter
Microwaves: Used for communication (satellites, WiFi, phones)
