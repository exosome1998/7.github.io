Practice: Heat Transfer üéØ

Quick Check

Q1: Why does a wooden spoon stay cooler than a metal spoon in hot tea?
Answer: Wood is a poor conductor (insulator). Its particles are less organized for heat transfer compared to metal's tightly packed particles.
Q2: A lava lamp has wax that rises when hot and sinks when cool. What heat transfer method is this?
Answer: Convection - Hot wax (less dense) rises, cool wax (more dense) sinks, creating a convection current.
Q3: Explain using particles why a metal rod conducts heat better than a glass rod.
Answer: Metal particles are very close and have free electrons that transfer energy quickly through collisions. Glass particles transfer energy more slowly.
Q4: Why can't a brick wall transfer heat by convection?
Answer: Solid particles are locked in fixed positions and cannot move to create currents. Only vibration (conduction) possible.
Q5: Compare how heat transfers in water vs ice using particle model.
Answer: Ice (solid): particles fixed, only conduction. Water (liquid): particles can move, both conduction AND convection possible.

Practice: Chemical Reactions üéØ

Quick Check

Q1: Ice melting feels cold to touch. Endothermic or exothermic?
Answer: Endothermic - Absorbs heat from surroundings (your hand), making it feel cold.
Q2: 50g zinc + 20g sulfur ‚Üí zinc sulfide. What mass of product?
Answer: 70g - Mass conserved: 50g + 20g = 70g
Q3: Why does sugar dissolve faster in hot water than cold water?
Answer: Higher temp ‚Üí particles move faster ‚Üí more frequent and energetic collisions ‚Üí faster dissolving.
Q4: Why does iron wool rust faster than an iron nail?
Answer: Iron wool has much larger surface area ‚Üí more iron exposed to oxygen ‚Üí more collisions ‚Üí faster rusting.
Q5: A glow stick releases light when bent. What type of reaction?
Answer: Exothermic - Chemical reaction releases energy as light (and some heat).
Q6: What's the role of a catalyst in a reaction?
Answer: Speeds up reaction by lowering activation energy. Not consumed - can be reused.
