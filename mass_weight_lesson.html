üß± What is Mass?

Mass is how much matter (stuff) is in an object.

(How much 'stuff' you're made of)

Key Points About Mass:

  • Measured in kilograms (kg)
  • Never changes - same everywhere in universe
  • You have the same mass on Earth, Moon, or Mars

‚öñÔ∏è What is Weight?

Weight is the force of gravity pulling on your mass.

(How hard gravity pulls you down)

Key Points About Weight:

  • Measured in Newtons (N)
  • Changes depending on gravity
  • Less gravity = less weight
  • More gravity = more weight

üéØ Remember This

You have the same mass everywhere, but your weight changes!

ü§î Mass vs Weight - The Difference

Property Mass Weight
What is it? Amount of matter Force of gravity
Units Kilograms (kg) Newtons (N)
Changes? No - always same Yes - depends on gravity
On Moon? Same as Earth Much less than Earth

Example: A 50 kg person has the same mass everywhere, but weighs 490 N on Earth and only 82 N on the Moon!

üåç What is Gravity?

Gravity is a force that pulls objects towards each other.

(Everything with mass pulls on everything else)

How Gravity Works:

  • Bigger objects have stronger gravity
  • Earth is massive, so it pulls strongly
  • Moon is smaller, so it pulls weaker
  • You also have gravity, but it's tiny!

üìê The Weight Formula

To calculate weight, we use a simple formula:

Weight = Mass √ó Gravity
W = m √ó g

What Each Letter Means:

  • W = Weight (in Newtons, N)
  • m = Mass (in kilograms, kg)
  • g = Gravity strength (in m/s¬≤)

üí° Memory Trick

"Weight equals Mass times Gravity" - WMG!
